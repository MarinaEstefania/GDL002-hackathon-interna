The Bechdel Test

Inspired by Alison Bechdel's 1985 comic strip "The rule", the Bechdel test has become a way to measure women's representation in a film.

The Bechdel Test, sometimes called the Movie Measure or Bechdel Rule is a simple test which names the following three criteria:

(1) It has to have at least two women in it, who (2) who talk to each other, about (3) something besides a man. The test was popularized by Alison Bechdel's comic Dykes to Watch Out For, in a 1985 strip called The Rule.
